# Sources & References

## Classical Persian Literature

### Primary Texts
- Ferdowsi. *Shahnameh (Book of Kings)* (c. 1010 CE). Epic Persian poem containing references to Persian mythology including supernatural beings.
  - Various translations available (Dick Davis translation recommended)
- Nizami Ganjavi. *Khamsa (The Quintet)* (12th century). Five long narrative poems including romantic epics.
- Hafez. *Divan-e Hafez* (14th century). Collection of lyrical poetry with mystical and mythological references.

### Literary Studies
- Davis, D. (2006). *Panthea's Children: Hellenistic Novels and Medieval Persian Romances*. Bibliotheca Iranica.
- Yarshater, E. (Ed.) (1983-present). *Encyclopædia Iranica*. Contains extensive entries on Persian mythology.

## Western Literary Introduction

### Thomas Moore
- Moore, T. (1817). *Lalla Rookh: An Oriental Romance*. Longman, Hurst, Rees, Orme, and Brown, London.
  - "Paradise and the Peri" — Famous poem introducing peri to Western audiences
  - Various modern editions and reprints available

### Romantic & Orientalist Literature

## Notes on Sources

### Translation Challenges

Many primary Persian texts exist in various translations with differing quality and scholarly rigor. Consulting multiple translations or original Persian texts (for those with language skills) is recommended for serious research.

### Cultural Context

Understanding peri requires knowledge of:
- Zoroastrian religious and philosophical concepts
- Persian literary traditions and symbolism
- Evolution from pre-Islamic to Islamic-era Persian culture
- Distinction between religious doctrine and folk belief
